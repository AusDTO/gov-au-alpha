The vision for GOV.AU Prototype

Building GOV.AU

The Digital Transformation Office’s GOV.AU Prototype has been built to meet the Digital Service Standards. Australian Government digital services must meet the standards to ensure government services delivered online are simpler, faster and easier to use.

The process

A team of developers, designers and researchers at the Digital Transformation Office had nine weeks to move through the phases of Discovery and Alpha to build and polish this prototype for a public release online.

The Research

The Discovery phase saw four weeks of user research take place to find out if it was possible to make government services so easy to use that people don’t need to know the structure of government to get things done.
As the discovery phase of user research progressed two fictional personas were introduced, to show what starting a business (Bobbi’s user journey) and the experience of coming to Australia (Haruki’s user journey) may look like on a new GOV.AU.

How we did it

The Alpha phase saw the build of GOV.AU take place over five weeks, sketched in code the prototype focuses on a single task around starting a business which eventually needs to hire overseas staff.

This prototype works by having a user answer a few simple questions upfront, it then matches the right government information (local, state and federal) to the user’s situation.

Checklists are then provided for the user to follow with links so they can explore further what they’ll need to do to start a task or find out more information.

Several Ministers’ and Department pages have been built to illustrate how different types of content could be rendered.The pages are not intended to be correct because they exist for illustrative purposes only.
